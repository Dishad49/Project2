Red-Bull: The story of a million dares

Oracle Red Bull Racing stands among F1's greatest dynasties—four consecutive Constructors' titles, multiple Drivers' Championships with Sebastian Vettel and Max Verstappen, and a record 15-race winning streak. Now with Max Verstappen and rising star Isack Hadjar, plus a bold new Ford-powered engine for 2026, Red Bull continues chasing motorsport greatness.

Essential drivers of Red-Bull



Max Verstappen

Four-time World Champion, the team's most successful driver ever, with 71+ Grand Prix wins

Sebastien Vettel

Won four consecutive titles with Red Bull, becoming the sport's youngest quadruple champion

Isack Hadjar

Promoted for 2026 as Verstappen's new teammate after an impressive rookie season with Racing Bulls

Daniel Ricciardo

Never won a title but was a fan favorite for his game, overtaking and race craft, picking up 7 wins while outpacing Merc

In 2012, teenage rivals Verstappen and Leclerc clashed in a WSK karting race. Leclerc pushed Verstappen wide to pass; Verstappen retaliated the same way a few corners later. Leclerc, angered, shoved Verstappen into a puddle post-race — both were disqualified. Verstappen later complained to reporters that it was unfair he got blamed for pushing back, while Leclerc dismissed it as "just an inchident."

"I'm leading, he wants to pass, he push me, I push him back, and after he push me off the track — it's not fair, right?"
-Max Verstappen (2012)
